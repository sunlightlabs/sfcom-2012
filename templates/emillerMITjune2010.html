List of sites cited by Ellen Miller, Executive Director, at MIT:

Databases

  • Open Secrets

    Campaign finance data and more - essential site for money and influence data. The Sunlight Foundation uses data from Open Secrets in many projects.

  • National Institute on Money In State Politics

    Reveals the influence of campaign money on state-level elections and public policy in all 50 states. The Sunlight Foundation uses data from Open Secrets in many projects.

  • Foreign Lobbying Influence Tracker

    Foreign Lobbyist Influence Tracker, a joint project of ProPublica and the Sunlight Foundation, digitizes information that representatives of foreign governments, political parties and government-controlled entities must disclose to the U.S. Justice Department when they seek to influence U.S. policy.

  • House Expenditure Reports Database

    The database contains expenditure records for all offices—members of Congress, legislative offices (like the House Clerk’s Office) and House committees. Prepared by the Sunlight Foundation.

  • White House Visitor Logs

    Acting on the online release of visitor data provided by the White House, Sunlight Labs mechanically matched the names of White House visitors to names in OpenSecrets.org, FollowTheMoney.org, LittleSis.org, Google and Wikipedia. Prepared by the Sunlight Foundation.

  • Taxpayers for Common Sense

    Taxpayers for Common Sense is an independent and non-partisan voice for taxpayers working to increase transparency and expose and eliminate wasteful and corrupt subsidies, earmarks, and corporate welfare.

  • Subsidy Scope

    Subsidyscope, launched by The Pew Charitable Trusts, aims to raise public awareness about the role of federal subsidies in the economy. The Sunlight Foundation is partner in this project.

  • Public.Resource.Org

    Public.Resource.Org is a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation dedicated to publishing and sharing public domain materials in the United States.[1] It was founded by Carl Malamud and is based out of Sebastopol, California. Their motto is “Making Government Information More Accessible”.

  • Department of Labor Enforcement Data

    This site aims to make the enforcement data, collected by these agencies in the exercise of their mission, accessible and searchable, using common search criteria, by the public. It intends, also, to engage the public in new and creative ways of using this data.

  • Mineral and Mining Services Data

    Not a great website but an invaluable source of data.

Tools

  • Little Sis

    LittleSis is an involuntary facebook of powerful Americans, collaboratively edited by people like you. They bring transparency to influential social networks by tracking the key relationships of politicians, corporate executives, lobbyists, financiers, and their affiliated organizations. Little Sis is a grantee of the Sunlight Foundation.

  • Real Time Congress iPhone App

    Real Time Congress is the fast and free way to access real-time information about Congress on your iPhone. This app puts the actions, meetings and documents that make up the legislative process right at your fingertips. Whether you're a journalist, Hill staffer, blogger or just an interested citizen, this app will keep you up to date on what's happening in Congress right now. Another innovative app from the non-partisan, non-profit Sunlight Labs.

  • Congress Android App

    Our Congress app takes power from the insiders on Capitol Hill and tucks it right into your pocket. Do your own real time research with this open-source congressional dictionary: read legislation, check up on the latest votes, track news about specific members of Congress, and find out what your representatives are saying on Twitter and YouTube. Don't be shy: you can tweet right back to them, or if you're feeling more ambitioustake full advantage of our Sunlight Labs Congress API and help us continue to build a better app.

  • Politiwidgets

    Up-to-date, embeddable political infographics from the Sunlight Foundation.

  • MapLight

    MAPLight.org, a groundbreaking public database, illuminates the connection between money and politics in unprecedented ways. Elected officials collect large sums of money to run their campaigns, and they often pay back campaign contributors with special access and favorable laws.

  • National Data Catalog

    The National Data Catalog is an open platform for government data sets and APIs. It makes it easy to find datasets by and about government, across all levels (federal, state, and local) and across all branches (executive, legislative, and judicial). The data here is imported from multiple sources and curated by the Sunlight Foundation.

  • Open Congress

    OpenCongress combines blogs, news, and social data with official government information to bring you the real story behind what's happening in Congress. Track the bills, votes, issues, and elected officials you care about, share this information with your community, and take action. Use our integrated platform to write directly to your representatives about the issues that matter to you. A joint project of the Sunlight Foundation and the Participatory Politics Foundation, OpenCongress is non-partisan, not-for-profit, and open-source.

  • Transparency Data

    Transparency Data is a central source for federal lobbyist registrations, federal grants, and federal and state campaign contributions. Here you can begin your search, find the information you need, and then download records of what a candidate has received, what an individual has given, how much an organization has spent on lobbying, and many other queries.

Media

  • Sunlight Foundation Reporting Group

    The Reporting Group applies the techniques of journalism to make the government more transparent. Their mission to is assess the accuracy, completeness and usefulness of federal data, to demonstrate through their reporting how this data can be used effectively and in real time, and to help other reporters do the same.

  • Sunlight Live

    Sunlight Live is an innovative new reporting platform that harnesses the power of live streaming video and real-time reporting to give coverage of live events in Congress more interactivity and depth. Watch with our team and you'll get live analysis of events with contextual government data right next to the video feed.

  • Party Time

    From the early hours of the morning until late in the evening, politicians are partying. Sunlight's PARTY TIME can help you find out who is partying, where and when.

Advocacy

  • Public Online Information Act of 2010

    This bill would require the Executive Branch to put all public government information online in a timely manner and in user-friendly formats. It would also create an advisory panel to develop government-wide disclosure policies following the principle that government information isn't "public" unless it is online. All information that is exempted from FOIA disclosure would be exempted from the disclosure requirements under this act as well.

  • DISCLOSE Act

    A bill to amend the Federal Election Campaign Act of 1971 to prohibit foreign influence in Federal elections, to prohibit government contractors from making expenditures with respect to such elections, and to establish additional disclosure requirements with respect to spending in such elections, and for other purposes.

  • Read The Bill: The 72 Hour Rule

    Amending the Rules of the House of Representatives to require that legislation and conference reports be available on the Internet for 72 hours before consideration by the House, and for other purposes.

  • Earmark Transparency Act

    A bill to require Congress to establish a unified and searchable database on a public website for congressional earmarks as called for by the President in his 2010 State of the Union Address to Congress

  • Transparency in Government Act

    To amend the Ethics in Government Act of 1978, the Rules of the House of Representatives, the Lobbying Disclosure Act of 1995, and the Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act of 2006 to improve access to information in the legislative and executive branches of the Government, and for other purposes.

  • Public=Online Campaign

    PUBLIC=ONLINE is a national, non-partisan campaign seeking to create a transparent government built on open data and information. Between now and November 2010, we are ensuring government transparency is a major issue in the mid-term elections by building a grassroots movement of citizens demanding that government information be made available online and in real-time. Funded by the Sunlight Foundation.
